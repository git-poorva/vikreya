5 Inventory Mistakes Killing Your Amazon Profits

Read Time: 3 Minutes

Inventory mismanagement is one of the biggest silent profit killers for Amazon sellers in India. Whether you’re doing ₹5L or ₹50L monthly revenue, poor stock planning leads to lost rankings, excess storage fees, and cash flow problems.

1. Overstocking Without Demand Forecasting

Ordering bulk inventory without sales data ties up capital and increases long-term FBA storage fees. Many sellers overestimate demand during peak months and get stuck with dead inventory.

Fix: Use 60–90 day rolling sales averages and adjust for seasonality before placing POs.

2. Understocking & Losing Ranking

Running out of stock hurts more than lost sales. Amazon’s algorithm penalizes inactive listings, and recovering rankings can take weeks.

Fix: Maintain a 30–45 day buffer stock based on average daily sales.

3. Ignoring Restock Lead Time

Many sellers calculate reorder quantity but forget supplier production + shipping time. This leads to avoidable stockouts.

Formula: Reorder Point = (Daily Sales × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

4. Not Monitoring Inventory Turnover

Low inventory turnover means cash is stuck. Healthy FBA businesses maintain turnover between 4–8 cycles annually.

Tip: Regularly remove slow-moving SKUs or bundle them to improve sell-through.

5. Ignoring Storage Fee Optimization

Long-term storage fees can destroy margins. Sellers often ignore aging inventory reports until fees spike.

Solution: Run promotions or ads before inventory hits 270–365 days.

Smart Inventory Strategy for Indian Sellers

  • Forecast monthly demand
  • Track sell-through weekly
  • Plan reorders 45–60 days in advance
  • Monitor aged inventory reports

Final Thoughts

Inventory optimization directly impacts Amazon FBA profitability. Sellers who track data and plan reorders scientifically outperform competitors consistently.
